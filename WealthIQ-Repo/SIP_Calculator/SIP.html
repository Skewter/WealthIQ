SIP Calculator – Systematic Investment Plan Calculator

Potential investors may mistakenly perceive SIPs and mutual funds as synonymous. However, SIPs are essentially a method of investing in mutual funds, with the alternative being a lump sum investment. A SIP calculator serves as a tool to help investors gauge the potential returns when allocating funds to such investment instruments. Systematic Investment Plan or SIP involves investing a fixed sum of money in mutual funds at regular intervals, typically allowing investments on a weekly, quarterly, or monthly basis.

What is a SIP Calculator?

A SIP calculator is a user-friendly tool designed to provide individuals with an estimate of the returns on their mutual fund investments made through SIP. SIP investments in mutual funds have gained popularity, especially among millennials, becoming a favored investment option.

These calculators are crafted to offer potential investors an approximation of their mutual fund investments. It's essential to note that the actual returns from a mutual fund scheme may vary based on different factors. The SIP calculator does not account for exit loads and expense ratios, if any.

This calculator determines the wealth gain and expected returns for monthly SIP investments. It offers a rough estimate of the maturity amount for any given monthly SIP, based on a projected annual return rate.

How Can a SIP Return Calculator Help You?

SIPs are considered a more attractive mode of investing funds compared to a lump sum, as per insights from several mutual fund experts. It aids in financial discipline and fosters a savings habit beneficial for future financial goals.

An online SIP calculator is a valuable tool providing an insight into the estimated returns following the investment tenure. Some benefits of SIP calculators include:

  • Assisting in determining the desired investment amount.
  • Displaying the total amount invested.
  • Offering an estimated value of the potential returns.

How Do SIP Calculators Work?

The SIP calculator operates on the following formula:

\[ M = P \times \left( \frac{[1 + i]^n – 1}{i} \times (1 + i) \right) \]

In this formula:

  • \( M \) is the amount received upon maturity.
  • \( P \) is the amount invested at regular intervals.
  • \( n \) is the number of payments made.
  • \( i \) is the periodic rate of interest.

For instance, if one invests Rs. 1,000 per month for 12 months at a periodic interest rate of 12%, the monthly rate of return is 12%/12 = 1/100 = 0.01. Hence, the formula yields approximately Rs. 12,809 in a year.

The rate of interest on a SIP can fluctuate based on market conditions, impacting the estimated returns.

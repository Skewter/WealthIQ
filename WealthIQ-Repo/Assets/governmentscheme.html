Government Scheme Information

1. Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY)

Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY) is a government-backed savings scheme launched by the Government of India under the "Beti Bachao, Beti Padhao" campaign. It aims to promote the welfare of the girl child by providing a long-term investment option for her future education and marriage expenses. Parents or legal guardians can open an SSY account for a girl child below the age of 10. The scheme offers attractive interest rates and tax benefits, with contributions eligible for deductions under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act.

2. National Pension System (NPS)

The National Pension System (NPS) is a voluntary retirement savings scheme launched by the Government of India. It aims to provide retirement income to individuals through market-based returns. Under the NPS, subscribers can contribute regularly during their working years and build a retirement corpus. The scheme offers two types of accounts: Tier-I, which is a mandatory long-term savings account with restrictions on withdrawals, and Tier-II, which is a voluntary savings account with more flexibility. NPS provides tax benefits under Section 80C and allows partial withdrawals for specific purposes after certain conditions are met.

3. Public Provident Fund (PPF)

The Public Provident Fund (PPF) is a long-term savings scheme established by the Government of India to encourage small savings among individuals. It offers a secure and tax-efficient investment avenue for building a retirement corpus. PPF accounts can be opened at designated banks and post offices, and contributions can be made annually, with a minimum and maximum limit set by the government. The scheme has a maturity period of 15 years, extendable in blocks of 5 years thereafter. PPF offers attractive interest rates, compounded annually, and tax benefits under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act.

4. National Savings Certificate (NSC)

The National Savings Certificate (NSC) is a popular government-sponsored savings scheme aimed at encouraging individuals to invest in small savings. It offers a fixed interest rate and can be purchased from designated post offices across India. NSC has a fixed maturity period, typically five or ten years, and provides investors with a safe and secure investment option. The interest earned on NSC is compounded annually and is eligible for tax benefits under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, making it an attractive choice for risk-averse investors.

5. Sovereign Gold Bond (SGB)

The Sovereign Gold Bond (SGB) scheme, introduced by the Government of India, allows investors to invest in gold in a paperless manner. It aims to reduce the demand for physical gold and promote financial savings. SGBs are issued by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) on behalf of the Government and are denominated in grams of gold. These bonds have a fixed tenure of typically eight years and offer investors both capital appreciation and fixed interest income. SGBs also provide tax benefits and can be traded on stock exchanges, offering liquidity to investors.
